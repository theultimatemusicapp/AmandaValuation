Understanding Valuation Methods

Revenue Multiplier

What: Multiplies your annual revenue (ARR) by a factor (e.g., 5-8).

Best for: SaaS businesses with stable, recurring revenue streams.

Example: $1M ARR with a 6x multiplier = $6M valuation.

Income-Based

What: Multiplies net profit by a factor (e.g., 4-7).

Best for: Profitable SaaS businesses with consistent earnings.

Example: $200K profit with a 5x multiplier = $1M valuation.

Earnings-Based

What: Combines ARR and profit, adjusted for growth.

Best for: Growing SaaS businesses with improving margins.

Example: $1M ARR, $200K profit = $1.5M valuation.

Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

What: Discounts future cash flows to present value.

Best for: SaaS businesses with long-term growth potential.

Example: $1M ARR, 20% growth = $3M valuation.
